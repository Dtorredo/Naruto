ABOUT THIS GUIDE
This user manual explains how to use the PreNatal Care Management System. It uses simple language and step-by-step tasks so non-technical users can complete common actions quickly.

-----------------------------------------------------------------

SYSTEM OVERVIEW
The PreNatal Care Management System helps clinics manage patient records, prenatal tracking, appointments, and checkups. Patients can access a personal portal to view their appointments and medical records after a staff member links their account.

Key Features:
1) Invite-only patient onboarding
2) Role-based access control (doctor, nurse, admin, patient)
3) Patient record management
4) Prenatal and checkup record tracking
5) Appointment scheduling
6) Patient portal for self-service access

-----------------------------------------------------------------

FAQs
Q: Why can’t I sign up as a patient?
A: The system is invite-only. A staff member must send you an invite.

Q: I logged in but see “Patient Record Pending.” What does that mean?
A: A staff member has not linked your account to a patient record yet.

Q: How do I resend an invite?
A: Staff can open Add Patient again and re-enter the email.

-----------------------------------------------------------------

MESSAGES AND THEIR MEANINGS
1) “Patient Record Pending” → Your account is not linked to a patient record yet.
2) “No patient record found” → The system cannot find your patient data. Contact clinic staff.
3) “Unauthorized” / “Forbidden” → You do not have access to the current page.

-----------------------------------------------------------------

TROUBLESHOOTING
1) Login fails → Check your email/password or reset your password.
2) No invite email received → Check spam/junk folder, then ask staff to resend.
3) Cannot view records → Confirm your account is linked to a patient record.

-----------------------------------------------------------------

GLOSSARY
1) RLS (Row Level Security): Database rule that limits who can view data.
2) EDD (Expected Delivery Date): The estimated date of delivery.
3) LMP (Last Menstrual Period): The first day of the last menstrual period.
